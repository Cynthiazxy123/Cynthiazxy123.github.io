SAMA: Factorized Semantic Anchoring and Motion Alignment for Instruction-Guided Video Editing
Xinyao Zhang1,2,*, Wenkai Dong2,*, Yuxin Song2,*,†, Bo Fang2,3, Qi Zhang2, Jing Wang1,2, Fan Chen2, Hui Zhang2, Haocheng Feng2, Yu Lu4,†, Hang Zhou2, Chun Yuan1, Jingdong Wang2
*Equal contribution   †Corresponding author
1Tsinghua University, 2Baidu Inc, 3City University of Hong Kong, 4Zhejiang University
We address the fundamental challenge in instruction-guided video editing: simultaneously achieving precise semantic modification and faithful motion preservation. SAMA factorizes video editing into two sub-tasks — Semantic Anchoring (jointly predicting semantic tokens and video latents on sparse anchor frames to establish reliable visual anchpoints) and Motion Alignment (pre-training on motion-centric video inpainting tasks including cube inpainting, speed perturbation, and tube shuffle). A two-stage training strategy enables learning from unpaired data in Stage 1, achieving strong zero-shot editing, before supervised fine-tuning in Stage 2. SAMA achieves state-of-the-art among open-source methods on VIE-Bench, OpenVE-Bench, and ReCo-Bench, competitive with commercial systems like Kling-Omni.

SuperVLAD: Compact and Robust Image Descriptors for Visual Place Recognition
(Co-author including Xinyao Zhang)
THU-CVML, Tsinghua University
Visual place recognition (VPR) under domain shifts remains challenging due to high-dimensional descriptors and distribution bias in traditional NetVLAD. SuperVLAD proposes a cluster-center-free framework that eliminates inter-domain distribution shift, achieving superior cross-domain generalization. With as few as 4 clusters, SuperVLAD matches or exceeds standard NetVLAD with many more clusters, significantly reducing descriptor dimensionality while achieving state-of-the-art performance on Pitts30k, MSLS-val, and multiple VPR benchmarks.

3GeM Pooling: Direction-Aware and Compact Global Descriptors for Visual Place Recognition
Xinyao Zhang et al.
THU-CVML, Tsinghua University
Standard GeM pooling treats all spatial locations uniformly, inadvertently incorporating non-discriminative regions (sky, pedestrians, dynamic objects) into global descriptors. We propose 3GeM Pooling, a direction-aware aggregation method that performs generalized mean pooling across three dimensions (H×W, H×D, W×D), capturing richer structural information including horizontal and vertical directional differences. The SGCE (Spatially Guided Channel Enhancement) module uses spatial cues to generate attention weights for channel features, boosting discriminability while maintaining compact 1024-dimensional representations. Achieves state-of-the-art on MSLS, Pitts30k, and other benchmarks with better generalization in low-data regimes.
